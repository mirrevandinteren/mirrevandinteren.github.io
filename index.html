Maps

The maps above were made in an attempt to visualize an indicator of a specific target of a Sustainable Development Goal. This goal revolves around protecting, restoring, and promoting the sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems. Specifically, these maps visualize Indicator 15.1.2, which measures the proportion of important sites for terrestrial and freshwater biodiversity covered by protected areas, categorized by ecosystem type. This sustainable development goal resonated deeply with me due to my background in environmental sciences and my passion for fostering a more sustainable world for everyone. These maps were created using QGIS.

Below, you can find an ArcGIS web application where you can use the slider tool to compare the freshwater and terrestrial biodiversity of different countries. By clicking on a country, you can see the exact percentage of important sites that are covered by protected areas.

If you want the app to work in full screen, you can click here.

The map above shows the agriculture, state of vegetation, and a true color composite of Kingston, ON Canada. Both the first half of the year (Januari through June) and the second half of the year (July through December) are visible if you turn on the right layer. You can zoom into the area and turn the layer of interest on. This application was made through Google Earth Engine.

User

Hello there, welcome to my page!

My name is Mirre van Dinteren. I'm a 23-year-old undergraduate student at Utrecht University, in the Netherlands. I'm very passionate about our Earth, how it behaves and functions, and I have a general appreciation for its beauty. That is the main reason why I chose my major to be in Earth Science and Mathematics. I have done several courses focused on geology, but also on Environmental Sciences.

My path towards Earth and Environmental Sciences has not been the most straightforward. I have studied Architecture and Building Sciences at the Technical University of Eindhoven for a year, but that was not quite the right fit for me. Despite the fact that I really liked the computational part of that program, it had too little contact to my sense of purpose in life. Afterwards, I have spend two years discovering what it is that does spark my interest, spark, and drive in life. I have travelled, and thereby enhanced my learning process regarding myself, and developed myself generally. When I started at University College Utrecht, I quickly found that the Earth is something that ticks all my boxes regarding my pleasure, purpose, and power.

During my University program, I took a course focused on Geographical Information Systems. I have learned to use an assortment of programs such as ArcGIS, QGIS, Google Earth Engine, and ArcGIS field maps. On this website, my portfolio from this course can be found.

Have a look around!

About

The picture above was taken by me in Wanaka, New Zealand in 2020.

This website was created as an online portfolio for a course on Geographical Information Systems. The main goal of this course was to understand the use of GIS for administrative purposes by governments, environmental monitoring, and land use planning. Through this course, I have acquired skills in basic GIS principles and cartography. I have also learned how to implement common operations using GIS, process publicly available spatial data, and use both industry standard and open-source GIS software. In addition, I gained experience in evaluating topological relationships between objects in space, modeling and representing them in a spatial data management system, and applying spatial statistics to analyze data from a field survey and report the results.

You can find the choropleth maps I made to visualize SDG indicator 15.1.2, together with a Google Earth Engine map, under the tab Maps.
